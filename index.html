California, USA

California is the most populous U.S. state and a land of extremes—from the sun-drenched beaches of the Pacific Coast to the snow-capped Sierra Nevada mountains. It’s home to Mount Whitney, the highest peak in the contiguous U.S., and Death Valley, the lowest point in North America—just 85 miles apart. Known for its innovation, California houses Silicon Valley, the global tech hub, and Hollywood, the heart of the entertainment industry. It became the 31st state in 1850, and its nickname, “The Golden State,” nods to both the 1849 Gold Rush and its iconic golden poppies, the state flower.
